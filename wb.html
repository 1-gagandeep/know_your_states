Geography and Demographics

Location: West Bengal is bordered by Bangladesh to the east, Nepal and Bhutan to the north, Assam to the northeast, Odisha to the southwest, Jharkhand and Bihar to the west, and Sikkim to the northwest.

Capital: Kolkata, the cultural and financial hub of eastern India.

Major Cities: Kolkata, Howrah, Siliguri, Durgapur, Asansol, Darjeeling.

Area: Approximately 88,752 square kilometers.

Population: Over 91 million.

History

Ancient Period: West Bengal's history dates back to the Mauryan Empire and the Gupta period. The region was also part of the Bengal Sultanate and a key center for Buddhism and Jainism in ancient India.

Medieval Period: The region was ruled by various Muslim rulers and eventually became part of the Mughal Empire. It was later colonized by the British, who established Calcutta (now Kolkata) as the capital of British India until 1911.

Colonial Period: West Bengal was the heart of the Indian independence movement. The partition of Bengal in 1905 and 1947 significantly shaped its socio-political landscape.

Culture and Language

Languages: Bengali is the official language and is widely spoken throughout the state. English is also commonly used, particularly in education and government.

Culture: West Bengal is known for its rich cultural heritage, including the classical dance form of Manipuri, the Rabindra Sangeet (songs written by Rabindranath Tagore), and its vibrant literary tradition. Major festivals include Durga Puja, Kali Puja, and Poila Baisakh (Bengali New Year).

Economy

Agriculture: West Bengal is a leading producer of rice, jute, and tea. The Darjeeling tea from West Bengal is globally famous.

Industries: The state has a diversified industrial base, including jute, textiles, information technology, and steel. Kolkata is a major industrial hub, particularly for the jute and tea industries.

Education and Institutions

Notable Institutions: West Bengal is home to renowned educational institutions like the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Calcutta, the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI), Jadavpur University, and the University of Calcutta.

Historical Significance: The University of Calcutta, founded in 1857, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in India. The state has a long tradition of intellectual and cultural movements, including the Bengal Renaissance.

Tourism

Historical Sites: West Bengal boasts iconic landmarks such as the Howrah Bridge, Victoria Memorial, and the Sundarbans (home to the Royal Bengal Tiger).

Natural Beauty: The state is known for its picturesque hill stations like Darjeeling, the Sundarbans mangrove forests, and scenic riverbanks along the Hooghly River.

Government and Politics

Current Chief Minister: Mamata Banerjee.

Current Governor: C.V. Ananda Bose.

Political Landscape: West Bengal has a vibrant political environment, with the All India Trinamool Congress (AITC) being the ruling party. The Communist Party of India (Marxist) and the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) are also significant players in state politics.
